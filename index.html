Future Plans

will

Spontaneous decision

(Dream, Prediction: will + verb)

- "Let's have a party!"
- "OK, I will buy drinks."

"I will become a pilot."
going to

Planned future action

(Intention: be + going to + verb)

"Mom! Dad! We are going to get married!"

"I am going to wash dishes."
Present Cont.

Future arrangement

(You know date and time: be + verb-ing)

"We are getting married in June!"

"I'm meeting my client at 3 pm."

Comparative Adjectives

Let's review how to form comparatives before emphasizing them.

Short Adjectives

Add -er
  • tall taller
  • nice nicer
  • big bigger
  • heavy heavier

Long Adjectives

Add more
  • expensive more
    expensive
  • beautiful more
    beautiful
  • intelligent more
    intelligent

Irregular

Memorize!
  • good better
  • bad worse
  • far farther

Don't forget to use

than

Emphasizing Comparatives

Big Difference

much / a lot / far

+ comparative

Small Difference

a bit / a little

+ comparative

No Difference

just as ... as

(No comparative suffix!)

Write sentences to emphasise comparatives. Use the hints given.

Big difference Superman / intelligent / the Joker
Superman is than the Joker.
Big difference Black Widow / careful / Harley Quinn
Black Widow is than Harley Quinn.
No difference Captain America / strong / Thor
Captain America is as Thor.
Small difference Spider-Man / fast / Captain America
Spider-Man is than Captain America.
No difference Thor / powerful / Superman
Thor is as Superman.
Small difference Batman / clever / Spider-Man
Batman is than Spider-Man.
